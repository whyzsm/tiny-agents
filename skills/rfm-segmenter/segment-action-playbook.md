# RFM Segment Action Playbook

## Detailed Campaign Strategies by Segment

---

## Champions (R4-5, F4-5, M4-5)

**Who they are:** Your best customers. Bought recently, buy often, and spend the most. They love your brand.

**Goal:** Keep them engaged, increase AOV, turn them into advocates.

**Do NOT:** Discount them. They're buying at full price already. A discount trains them to wait for offers.

### Campaign Ideas
1. **Early access program** — "You're one of our top customers. Here's access to our new collection 48 hours before everyone else."
2. **VIP event or behind-the-scenes content** — Factory tour, founder Q&A, product development preview.
3. **Referral program invitation** — Champions have the highest brand affinity and are most likely to refer successfully. Offer a generous referral reward.
4. **Bundle or curated set offer** — Higher AOV items: "Based on your purchases, we think you'd love this set."
5. **Personalized thank-you** — Handwritten note or personal email from the founder. High-retention, zero discount cost.

### Metrics to Track
- AOV trend (goal: growing)
- Referral conversion rate
- Segment retention rate quarter-over-quarter (goal: >85% remain in Champions or Loyal)

---

## Loyal Customers (R3-5, F3-5, M3-5)

**Who they are:** Consistent buyers with above-average spend. The backbone of recurring revenue.

**Goal:** Solidify loyalty, prevent downward migration, nudge toward Champions.

### Campaign Ideas
1. **Loyalty points or cashback program** — Reward purchase frequency directly.
2. **Cross-sell to adjacent categories** — "You love [Product A], you might like [Category B]."
3. **Anniversary or milestone messages** — "You've been a customer for 1 year!" (creates emotional connection, often no offer needed).
4. **Subscription upsell** — If you offer subscriptions, these customers are ideal candidates for auto-replenishment.
5. **New product beta testing** — "As one of our best customers, would you test this before launch?"

### Metrics to Track
- Churn rate to Needs Attention (goal: <10% per quarter)
- Cross-category purchase rate
- Subscription conversion rate

---

## Potential Loyalists (R4-5, F2-3, M2-3)

**Who they are:** Recent buyers with growing engagement. On the trajectory to loyalty if nurtured.

**Goal:** Accelerate second and third purchase to build habitual buying.

### Campaign Ideas
1. **Second purchase incentive** — "Thank you for your first order. Here's 10% off your next." (Second purchase is the most predictive moment for LTV.)
2. **Product education email series** — Help them get more value from what they bought. Reduces returns and increases re-purchase intent.
3. **Complementary product recommendation** — Pair their first purchase with a logical follow-on item.
4. **Review request with incentive** — "Leave a review and get $5 off." Turns them into advocates while deepening engagement.
5. **Wishlist feature promotion** — "Save items for later" creates future purchase intent data.

### Metrics to Track
- 90-day repeat purchase rate (industry benchmark: 25–35% for most categories)
- Time-to-second-purchase trend
- Segment upgrade rate to Loyal Customers

---

## New Customers (R4-5, F1)

**Who they are:** Just made their first (or second) purchase. The most critical onboarding window.

**Goal:** Convert to repeat buyers before they forget about you.

### Email Sequence (Days 1, 4, 7, 14, 30)
- **Day 1:** Order confirmation + brand story (why you exist)
- **Day 4:** Getting the most out of your purchase (product tips, care, usage)
- **Day 7:** Social proof — reviews and community content from similar customers
- **Day 14:** Cross-sell recommendation + 10% second-purchase offer
- **Day 30:** Check-in — "How are you enjoying [product]?" + review request

### Metrics to Track
- 30-day repeat rate
- 90-day repeat rate
- Onboarding email open rate by step

---

## Needs Attention (R2-3, F2-3, M2-3)

**Who they are:** Were buying regularly but recency is declining. They're distracted, not necessarily lost.

**Goal:** Re-engage before they slide into At Risk.

### Campaign Ideas
1. **"We noticed you haven't visited recently"** — Light personalization, no heavy discount yet.
2. **New arrivals since their last visit** — "A lot has changed since you were here last."
3. **Seasonal relevance hook** — "It's [season], perfect time to restock/refresh."
4. **Limited-time offer (10–15%)** — Creates urgency without being a desperate win-back.
5. **Browse abandonment retargeting** — If they've visited recently without converting, hit them with dynamic ads.

### Metrics to Track
- Response rate to re-engagement campaigns
- Segment migration: Needs Attention → Active vs. Needs Attention → At Risk

---

## At Risk (R1-2, F3-5, M3-5)

**Who they are:** Were Champions or Loyal Customers but haven't bought in a long time. Highest priority for win-back.

**Goal:** Reactivate before they become "Can't Lose Them" (even worse) or "Lost."

### Win-Back Sequence (3 emails over 2 weeks)
- **Email 1:** "We miss you" — Emotional, no offer. Show them what's new.
- **Email 2:** "A special offer just for you" — 15–20% discount + free shipping. Urgency: 7 days.
- **Email 3:** "Last chance" — Remind them the offer expires. Mention popular products they haven't tried.

### Additional Tactics
- **SMS win-back** — Higher open rate than email. Keep it short: "Hey [Name], it's been a while. Here's 20% off — expires Sunday."
- **Retargeting ads** — Show them their past purchases or complementary products.
- **Personalized reason to return** — "Your favorite [Product] is back in stock" or "New version of [Product] just launched."

### Metrics to Track
- Win-back email conversion rate (goal: 10–18%)
- Revenue recovered per reactivated customer
- Post-reactivation repeat purchase rate

---

## Can't Lose Them (R1, F4-5, M4-5)

**Who they are:** Were your most valuable customers. Haven't bought in the longest time. Extremely high LTV — worth a significant recovery investment.

**Goal:** Reactivate at any reasonable cost. Losing these customers permanently is very expensive.

### High-Touch Approach
1. **Personal outreach** — Email from the founder/CEO (not a mass blast template). "I noticed you haven't ordered recently and wanted to reach out personally."
2. **VIP reactivation offer** — 20–25% discount + free shipping + bonus gift (if applicable). The recovery cost is worth it given their historical LTV.
3. **Phone call or SMS** — For your very top 20–30 customers in this segment, a personal call is appropriate.
4. **Survey: "What happened?"** — Understanding why they left helps you fix the underlying issue for future customers.

### Metrics to Track
- Reactivation rate (goal: 20–35%)
- Post-reactivation LTV trajectory (do they return to Champions or just make one purchase?)

---

## Hibernating (R1-2, F1-2, M1-2)

**Who they are:** Low scores across all dimensions. Low investment value for recovery.

**Goal:** Minimal spend. One quarterly attempt, then suppress.

### Low-Cost Tactics
- **Annual "Still interested?" email** — Simple, no heavy creative. A 3–5% response rate is typical.
- **Category migration email** — If you've added new product lines since they last visited, show them.
- **Unsubscribe offer** — Let them opt out cleanly; this keeps your list healthy and email metrics accurate.

---

## Lost (R1, F1-2, M1-2)

**Who they are:** The weakest customers across all dimensions who haven't bought in the longest time.

**Goal:** Remove from active marketing. Do not invest in reactivation.

### Actions
- Move to suppression list (stop sending marketing emails)
- Retain in database for reporting
- Re-include in GDPR/CCPA data deletion processes if requested
- Optional: one "farewell" email + opt-out confirmation to clean list hygiene
